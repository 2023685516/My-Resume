DEEJA | EXPERIENCES


English Day Carnival — Form 1

In Form 1, my classmates and I took the initiative to run our own booth for the English Day Carnival. We carefully designed a word-syllable matching game that surprisingly became one of the liveliest and most engaging activities at the event. Throughout the process, I learned how to plan and manage a booth effectively, communicate and interact with a variety of visitors, and coordinate responsibilities within a team. This experience not only taught me organizational skills but also gave me a glimpse into leadership and the importance of creating activities that are both educational and enjoyable.

Sports House Marching — Form 2

During Form 2, I had the honor of representing my Green Sports House in the school marching event. While the performance may have seemed simple to an outside observer, the preparation involved hours of disciplined practice, coordination with my teammates, and consistent effort to perfect every step and formation. This experience significantly enhanced my understanding of precision, timing, and commitment. I also learned how collaborative teamwork can turn individual effort into a unified and impressive group performance, reinforcing both patience and perseverance.

School Kiosk Part-Time — Form 2

In the same year, I also worked part-time at my school’s kiosk. The role required me to interact with customers daily, handle small cash transactions accurately, manage inventory, and maintain the kiosk’s cleanliness and order. Although the job seemed simple on the surface, it was a great opportunity to develop practical skills such as customer service, responsibility, and effective communication. It also helped me gain confidence in handling real-world situations and strengthened my ability to remain professional while multitasking.

Jati Diri Camp — Form 2

I also participated in the Jati Diri Camp at Berhulu Camp in Jempol, Negeri Sembilan. This camp was one of the most memorable and transformative experiences of my school life. It pushed me physically and mentally through challenging outdoor activities, team-building exercises, and leadership tasks. I learned valuable lessons in responsibility, discipline, resilience, and teamwork. The camp also helped me understand the importance of supporting my peers while being self-aware and confident in my own abilities. By the end of the program, I felt more capable, independent, and prepared to face future challenges.

District-Level Marching & Sergeant Promotion — Form 3

In Form 3, I competed in a district-level marching event aimed at rank promotion. The training was intensive and required unwavering dedication, as every detail of our movements and formations had to be precise. After weeks of practice, hard work, and teamwork, I successfully earned the rank of Sergeant. This accomplishment was more than just a recognition; it strengthened my leadership abilities, taught me how to guide and motivate others, and helped me develop confidence under pressure. It was a milestone that highlighted the importance of discipline, perseverance, and strategic planning.

Leadership Camp — Form 5

In Form 5, I was selected to join a leadership camp specifically designed for school leaders, including class monitors, assistant monitors, and other elected positions. The camp provided an intensive learning environment where I gained skills in task management, effective communication, and problem-solving within a team. Through workshops, collaborative projects, and practical exercises, I learned how to prioritize responsibilities, manage group dynamics, and handle challenges thoughtfully and efficiently. This experience solidified my ability to lead, collaborate, and make well-considered decisions.

Company Marching (KOR) — Semester 1, UiTM

After completing high school, I continued my passion for marching by joining the company marching team for KOR during my first semester at UiTM. Representing Foxtrot Company, I engaged in months of rigorous training that tested my endurance, focus, and teamwork. The culmination of our effort was achieving 1st place in the competition, a reward for countless hours of practice and dedication. This experience reinforced my appreciation for discipline, the value of teamwork, and the satisfaction that comes from seeing sustained effort translate into real achievements. It also strengthened my commitment to personal growth and leadership in collaborative environments.

Internship at MPHS — Semester 4

During my Semester 4 break, I completed my internship at MPHS under the Enforcement Department. This placement exposed me to various administrative and field-related tasks. I learned how to manage and organize official documents, assist with enforcement operations, handle public inquiries, and understand the workflow behind municipal-level enforcement. The experience strengthened my communication skills, attention to detail, and ability to work in a structured, fast-paced environment.
